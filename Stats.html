Stats



Stats: In Grimline, the term "stats" refers to different attributes and characteristics of players in the game. These stats determine how powerful, effective, and capable a character is. Stats can include Health (HP), Mana (MP), Physical Strength (PHY STR), Magic Strength (MG STR), Physical Defense (PHY DEF), and Magical Defense (MG DEF).







"Health (HP): This stat indicates how many hits an individual can take before being defeated. Current HP is displayed in parentheses next to the Health stat. Remember that Health can be replenished through healing effects and items."



"Mana (MP): This stat shows the amount of magical energy a person has available for casting magic spells. The additional mana bar in parentheses shows the current available mana. Keep in mind that mana can be restored through using potions or waiting for a natural regen over time."



"Physical Strength (PHY STR): This stat determines how powerful a physical attack will be. It's measured in percentages, with higher values meaning stronger attacks. The attack power of weapons, such as swords, is influenced by this stat. For example, if a weapon has an attack that does 500 damage normally, and the user's PHY STR is 200, the attack will then inflict 1,500 damage."



"Magic Strength (MG STR): This stat determines the power of magic attacks. It's measured in percentages, with higher values leading to more devastating spells. When the user is wielding a magic weapon such as a wand, the DMG of that weapon is also influenced by this stat. For instance, if a wand has an attack that normally does 500 damage, and the user's MG STR is 200, the spell will then inflict 1,500 DMG."



"Physical Defense (PHY DEF): This stat reduces the amount of physical damage taken by a certain percentage. For example, if you have a PHY DEF of 25 and get hit by a physical attack that would normally deal 500 DMG, your defense will reduce the DMG to 375, resulting in less damage taken."



"Magic Defense (MG DEF): This stat reduces the amount of magic damage taken by a percentage. If a magic attack would deal 500 DMG and you have a MG DEF of 25, your defense will reduce the DMG to 375, minimizing the damage taken."
